# Overview

The AMXP protocol defines how supply-side platforms, demand-side bidders, and auditing agents communicate intent, enforce policy, and settle value. This chapter summarizes guiding principles such as interoperability by default, transparency, and verifiability.

## Guiding Principles

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Interoperability" icon="arrows-rotate">
    JSON-based payloads with explicit versioning keep integrations predictable.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Extensibility" icon="puzzle-piece">
    Optional sections allow experimentation without fragmenting the ecosystem.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Safety" icon="shield-check">
    Every transport includes authentication, encryption, and replay protection requirements.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
